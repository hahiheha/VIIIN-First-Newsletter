Quick Summary

Vietnam's impact investing ecosystem is gaining momentum as Q1 2026 closes: a landmark blended-finance facility launches, the government finalises its Social Enterprise framework, and we welcome seven new VIIN members across agri-tech, EdTech, and climate sectors. Our April Members Meeting will spotlight early-stage deal flow and co-investment opportunities — register below.

 

From Leadership

Message from the Executive Committee

Nguyen Thi Lan Anh

Executive Director · MYSC

Dear VIIN community,

As we enter the second quarter of 2026, it is clear that the hard work of the past three years is bearing fruit. Investor confidence in Vietnam's impact sector has never been higher — international LPs are asking better questions, and our portfolio companies are answering with data. This month we are particularly excited to host our bi-monthly Members Meeting focused on co-investment pathways, and to welcome a wave of new members who bring deep expertise in climate adaptation and inclusive fintech. Together, we continue to build an ecosystem worthy of the challenges ahead. Thank you for being part of it.

News & Insights

Policy

01

Vietnam Finalises Social Enterprise Legal Framework

The Ministry of Planning & Investment has published the final decree on Social Enterprise certification, setting out criteria for impact verification and tax incentives. Enterprises may apply from 1 June 2026, with the first cohort of certified SEs expected by year-end.

Read more →

Market

02

USD 40M Mekong Agri-Climate Facility Closes First Tranche

A blended-finance vehicle co-anchored by the Asian Development Bank and a consortium of impact investors has closed its first USD 15M tranche, targeting climate-resilient smallholder supply chains in the Mekong Delta. Three Vietnamese agri-tech firms are already in the pipeline for disbursement.

Read more →

Thought Leadership

03

Beyond the Term Sheet: Measuring Additionality in Southeast Asian Impact Deals

A new report from Beacon Fund and Fulbright University argues that deal-level additionality metrics — not just portfolio-wide IRIS+ scores — should become standard practice for SEA impact funds. The paper proposes a practical three-question framework applicable at term sheet stage.
